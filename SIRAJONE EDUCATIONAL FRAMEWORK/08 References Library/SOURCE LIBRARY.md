## PURPOSE

This document is the master research catalogue for the OUR LEGACY series. It records every source used or available for research — with complete bibliographic data, access instructions, reliability assessment, usage rules, and future research notes.

A researcher who has never worked on this project should be able to identify the right source for any Sīrah claim, know where to find it, and know how to use it correctly.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CATALOGUE

Sources are listed by tier. Within each tier, sources are listed in order of research priority — reach for the first source in each tier before the second.

Each entry contains the following fields (all required — record "Unknown" where not established):

- **Arabic Title** — full Arabic name
- **English Title** — transliterated or translated name
- **Author** — full name with dates (AH and CE)
- **Death Date** — year of author's death (AH / CE)
- **Publisher** — publisher of the edition used
- **Edition** — edition or print run
- **Publication Year** — year published or last reprinted
- **PDF Available?** — yes/no and URL or source
- **Physical Copy?** — yes/no and location if known
- **Shelf Location** — physical or digital file location (update when known)
- **Tier** — 1, 2, or 3
- **Reliability** — brief assessment of the source's scholarly standing
- **Primary Usage** — the main purpose this source serves in OUR LEGACY
- **Secondary Usage** — supplementary purposes
- **When NOT to Use** — explicit guidance on misuse
- **Lessons Cited** — which lesson numbers cite this source
- **Key Passages / Page References** — specific pages or passages used in current lessons
- **Arabic Quotations Preserved** — key Arabic phrases that appear in the lessons from this source
- **Future Research Ideas** — open questions this source could help answer

### T1-001 — AL-QURʾĀN

**Arabic Title:** الْقُرْآن الْكَرِيم
**English Title:** Al-Qur'ān al-Karīm (The Noble Qur'ān)
**Author:** The Word of Allah revealed to Muḥammad ﷺ via Jibrīl ʿalayhi al-salām
**Death Date:** N/A — divine revelation
**Publisher:** Multiple; Arabic Madinah Mushaf (King Fahd Complex) is the reference standard
**Edition:** Hafs ʿan ʿĀṣim recitation (standard in most Muslim-majority countries)
**Publication Year:** The Madinah Mushaf has been in continuous print since 1985
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Tanzil.net (Arabic, fully diacritised); QuranEncrypt (multiple translations)
**Physical Copy?:** Yes — multiple copies available
**Shelf Location:** Always accessible. Primary online reference: tanzil.net (Arabic) and corpus.quran.com (morphological)
**Tier:** 1
**Reliability:** The Qur'ān is the primary source of Islam and the ultimate authority in this project. Its text is mutawātir (transmitted by so many chains at every generation that fabrication is impossible). No claim in the Qur'ān requires hedging.
**Primary Usage:** Virtue anchors in SegmentPages (Qur'ānic verse cited for each virtue); Memory Gems where a Qur'ānic verse is the lesson's Memory Gem; establishing the theological and moral frame of each lesson.
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-referencing historical events mentioned in the Qur'ān (e.g., Sūrat al-Fīl on the Year of the Elephant; Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā on the orphan experience).
**When NOT to Use:** The Qur'ān is not a biographical source. Do not use it for chronological narrative detail, dates, names, or biographical facts not explicitly mentioned in the Qur'ān itself. Do not paraphrase Qur'ānic verses without clearly marking them as translations.
**Lessons Cited:** L1, L2, L3, L4, L5, L6, L7, L8, L9, L10 (and all future lessons)
**Key Passages / Page References:**
- Sūrat al-Fīl 105:1–5 — Year of the Elephant (L3)
- Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā 93:6–8 — Orphan experience (L6, L7, L10)
- Sūrat Quraysh 106:1–2 — Trade journeys (L8)
- Sūrat al-Sharḥ 94:1–3 — Opening of the chest (L5)
- Sūrat al-Tawbah 9:119 — Truthfulness (L10, Segment 1)
- Sūrat al-Rūm 30:21 — Marriage as divine sign (L10, Segment 2)
- Sūrat al-Baqarah 2:187 — Loyalty in marriage (L10, Segment 3)
- Sūrat al-ʿAlaq 96:1–5 — First Revelation (L11, planned)
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:**
- أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ (105:1)
- أَلَمْ يَجِدْكَ يَتِيمًا فَآوَىٰ (93:6)
- اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ (96:1)
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Identify all Qur'ānic verses that directly reference Meccan-period events not yet covered in L1–L10 (for L11–L20 planning).
- Compile a "Sīrah Verses" reference list — Qur'ānic passages with a direct historical referent in the Sīrah.

### T1-002 — ṢAḤĪḤ AL-BUKHĀRĪ

**Arabic Title:** الجامع المسند الصحيح المختصر من أمور رسول الله ﷺ وسننه وأيامه
**English Title:** Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (The Authentic Collection of al-Bukhārī)
**Author:** Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl al-Bukhārī (194–256 AH / 810–870 CE)
**Death Date:** 256 AH / 870 CE
**Publisher:** Darussalam (English); multiple Arabic publishers
**Edition:** 9-volume English edition (Muhammad Muhsin Khan translation) is the standard reference; Arabic editions from Dār Ṭawq al-Najāh are scholarly
**Publication Year:** Various; Darussalam English edition: 1997 (multiple reprints)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — sunnah.com (free, searchable, Arabic + English); archive.org for various editions
**Physical Copy?:** Available at major Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online: sunnah.com/bukhari — search by book or ḥadīth number
**Tier:** 1
**Reliability:** The most rigorously authenticated ḥadīth collection in Islamic scholarship. Al-Bukhārī applied the strictest isnād criteria of any classical muḥaddith. Ḥadīth in Bukhārī are accepted as ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) by the overwhelming consensus of Islamic scholarship across all schools.
**Primary Usage:** Memory Gems — every Companion's Memory Gem is verified in Bukhārī first. Authenticated statements of the Prophet ﷺ about Khadījah (3816), about the First Revelation (3), about his lineage. Virtue hadith for Segment anchors where a Bukhārī narration is available.
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-referencing Sīrah accounts from Ibn Hishām (T2) against authenticated narrations in Bukhārī where both discuss the same event.
**When NOT to Use:** Do not use sunnah.com to "confirm" a ḥadīth — use it to verify. Search for the ḥadīth text, not just a number someone else cited. Never cite "Bukhārī" for a narration not actually found in Bukhārī.
**Lessons Cited:** L1, L5, L6, L9, L10 (explicitly); general T1 authority for all lessons
**Key Passages / Page References:**
- Ḥadīth 3 — First Revelation (ʿĀʾishah's narration) — Primary source for L11
- Ḥadīth 3816 — "She believed in me when people disbelieved" — L10 Memory Gem
- Ḥadīth 3817 — Khadījah's virtue — L10 Companion
- Ḥadīth 3818–3820 — Additional narrations on Khadījah — L10 reference
- Ḥadīth 1359 — Related to the nursing period — L4 reference
- Ḥadīth 3748 — The Quraysh — L2 reference
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:**
- آمَنَتْ بِي إِذْ كَفَرَ النَّاسُ (from 3816 — "She believed in me when people disbelieved")
- اقْرَأْ (from Ḥadīth 3 — Jibrīl's first command)
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Compile complete list of Bukhārī narrations relevant to the Meccan period (L11–L20 content).
- For each future lesson, search Bukhārī's Book of al-Manāqib (Virtues and Merits) and Book of the Prophet's Biography for relevant authenticated narrations before using Ibn Hishām as primary.

### T1-003 — ṢAḤĪḤ MUSLIM

**Arabic Title:** المسند الصحيح المختصر بنقل العدل عن العدل إلى رسول الله ﷺ
**English Title:** Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim
**Author:** Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj al-Qushayrī al-Naysābūrī (206–261 AH / 821–875 CE)
**Death Date:** 261 AH / 875 CE
**Publisher:** Darussalam (English, 7-volume Nasiruddin al-Khattab translation); multiple Arabic publishers
**Edition:** Darussalam English edition; Arabic editions from Dār Iḥyāʾ al-Turāth al-ʿArabī
**Publication Year:** Darussalam English: 2007 (multiple reprints)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — sunnah.com/muslim (free, searchable, Arabic + English)
**Physical Copy?:** Available at major Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online: sunnah.com/muslim
**Tier:** 1
**Reliability:** The second-most rigorously authenticated ḥadīth collection. Muslim's methodology was slightly different from Bukhārī's — he prioritised chains where the narrators had definite personal contact (liqa). Both Ṣaḥīḥayn together (mutafaq ʿalayhi narrations) represent the highest possible level of ḥadīth authentication.
**Primary Usage:** The opening of the chest — Ḥadīth 162 (Anas ibn Mālik's narration) is the primary T1 source for the event narrated in L5. The Prophet ﷺ visiting his mother's grave — Ḥadīth 976 — used in L6 Companion.
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-referencing Bukhārī on shared narrations. Virtue ḥadīth where Muslim has a narration Bukhārī does not include.
**When NOT to Use:** Same rule as Bukhārī — verify on sunnah.com before citing.
**Lessons Cited:** L5 (ḥadīth 162), L6 (ḥadīth 976), general T1 authority for all lessons
**Key Passages / Page References:**
- Ḥadīth 162 — Opening of the chest (Anas ibn Mālik) — L5 primary source
- Ḥadīth 976 — Prophet ﷺ weeping at his mother's grave — L6 Companion
- Ḥadīth 2999 — The Prophet's lineage
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:**
- حَظَّ الشَّيْطَانِ مِنْكَ (from Muslim 162 — "the share of Shaytan from you")
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Muslim's ḥadīth on the Isrāʾ wal-Miʿrāj (Night Journey) will be the primary T1 source for that future lesson.
- Muslim's ḥadīth on the early revelations and the fatrah (gap between revelations) are relevant for L11 research.

### T2-001 — SĪRAT IBN HISHĀM

**Arabic Title:** السيرة النبوية
**English Title:** Sīrat Ibn Hishām (The Prophetic Biography — Ibn Hishām's Recension)
**Author:** ʿAbd al-Malik ibn Hishām al-Maʿāfirī (d. 218 AH / 833 CE) — editing the original Sīrah of Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq (d. 150 AH / 767 CE)
**Death Date (Ibn Hishām):** 218 AH / 833 CE; (Ibn Isḥāq): 150 AH / 767 CE
**Publisher (Arabic):** Dār al-Jīl, Beirut; Maktabat al-Tijāriyyah al-Kubrā, Cairo
**Publisher (English):** Oxford University Press — Alfred Guillaume translation: "The Life of Muhammad"
**Edition:** Multiple; Guillaume's English translation (OUP, 1955) is the standard English academic reference
**Publication Year:** Arabic: multiple editions; English: 1955 (multiple reprints through OUP)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Guillaume's English translation widely available as PDF; Arabic text at al-Maktaba al-Shāmila
**Physical Copy?:** Guillaume English: widely available at university libraries and Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online Arabic: al-Maktaba al-Shāmila (shamela.ws); Online English: search archive.org or university library systems
**Tier:** 2
**Reliability:** The earliest surviving comprehensive biography of the Prophet ﷺ. Widely accepted by classical scholarship as the primary Sīrah source for the Meccan period. However, it is not a ḥadīth collection with complete isnāds — it is a historical narrative work. Individual narrations vary in strength. The broad historical framework is accepted; individual claims require T1 corroboration where possible.
**Primary Usage:** The primary narrative source for ALL Meccan-period lessons (L2–L10). Used for: birth narrative, childhood events, nursing period, trade journeys, Baḥīrā encounter, Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl, marriage to Khadījah, taḥannuth period.
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-reference for Companion Key Facts sections; basis for Timeline tab entries in BookDetail.
**When NOT to Use:** Do not use Ibn Hishām alone (without T1 corroboration) for a claim that has theological implications (e.g., the nature of prophethood, the status of the Prophet's relatives). Do not treat Ibn Hishām's narrations as equivalent in authority to Bukhārī or Muslim.
**Lessons Cited:** L1, L2, L3, L4, L5, L6, L7, L8, L9, L10 — ALL Meccan-period lessons
**Key Passages / Page References (Guillaume English translation):**
- pp. 1–68 — Pre-Islamic Arabia, Quraysh lineage, ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (L2, L7)
- pp. 20–30 — Year of the Elephant, Abraha's expedition (L3)
- pp. 68–80 — Birth narrative, Āminah, ʿAbdullāh (L3, L6)
- pp. 80–90 — Ḥalīmah al-Saʿdiyyah and the nursing period (L4)
- pp. 82–84 — Opening of the chest (first occurrence) (L5)
- pp. 101–107 — Journey to Shām and Baḥīrā (L8)
- pp. 111–116 — Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl (L9)
- pp. 119–125 — Trade journey to Shām; Khadījah; proposal; marriage (L10)
- pp. 130–135 — taḥannuth and approach to the First Revelation (L10, L11)
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:**
- Ibn Hishām's phrase on Khadījah: "فكانت خديجة أول من آمن بالله ورسوله" (Khadijah was the first to believe in Allah and His Messenger)
**Future Research Ideas:**
- The full Baḥīrā account in Guillaume pp. 79–81 has more detail than used in L8; consider whether additional detail serves future lessons.
- Ibn Hishām's account of the taḥannuth (solitary retreat to Ḥirāʾ) in pp. 130–135 is the primary narrative for the pre-Revelation period — key source for L11.

### T2-002 — AL-ṬABARĪ — TĀRĪKH AL-RUSUL WAL-MULŪK

**Arabic Title:** تاريخ الرسل والملوك
**English Title:** The History of the Prophets and Kings (Tārīkh al-Ṭabarī)
**Author:** Muḥammad ibn Jarīr al-Ṭabarī (224–310 AH / 839–923 CE)
**Death Date:** 310 AH / 923 CE
**Publisher (Arabic):** Dār al-Maʿārif, Cairo (19 volumes, standard academic edition)
**Publisher (English):** SUNY Press — "The History of al-Tabari" (40 volumes)
**Edition:** SUNY Press English: various volumes, various translators
**Publication Year:** Arabic: 1960s onwards (Dār al-Maʿārif); English: 1987–2007 (SUNY Press)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Arabic text at al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; some SUNY volumes available at archive.org
**Physical Copy?:** SUNY Press volumes available at university libraries
**Shelf Location:** Online Arabic: al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; SUNY English: Volume 6 (Muhammad at Mecca) is the primary relevant volume
**Tier:** 2
**Reliability:** The most comprehensive Islamic historical chronicle. Al-Ṭabarī transmits narrations with isnāds (chains of transmission) but includes a very wide range of accounts, explicitly noting that he transmits what has been narrated rather than evaluating reliability. His method is inclusive rather than selective. The broad framework is reliable; individual narrations require caution.
**Primary Usage:** Cross-reference and supplementary narrative source for the Meccan period. Used when Ibn Hishām is silent on a detail or when al-Ṭabarī provides corroboration.
**Secondary Usage:** Additional context for pre-Islamic Arabian history (relevant to L2); the Year of the Elephant narrative (L3 cross-reference).
**When NOT to Use:** Do not use al-Ṭabarī's individual narrations as primary evidence for any claim without checking whether Ibn Hishām corroborates them. Al-Ṭabarī explicitly includes weak and contradictory narrations as part of his encyclopedic method.
**Lessons Cited:** L2, L3, L4, L6, L7, L8, L9, L10
**Key Passages / Page References (SUNY Volume 6 — Muhammad at Mecca):**
- pp. 1–40 — Pre-Islamic Arabian background (L2 cross-reference)
- pp. 41–50 — Year of the Elephant (L3 cross-reference)
- pp. 51–70 — Birth and childhood (L3, L4 cross-reference)
- pp. 71–80 — Trade journeys and Khadījah (L10 cross-reference)
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:** None unique to al-Ṭabarī used in current lessons; he largely transmits the same accounts as Ibn Hishām.
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Al-Ṭabarī's account of the early Madīnan period will be a critical cross-reference for L11 onwards (the First Revelation, the early persecutions, the Hijrah).

### T2-003 — IBN SAʿD — AL-ṬABAQĀT AL-KUBRĀ

**Arabic Title:** الطبقات الكبرى
**English Title:** The Major Classes (al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrā)
**Author:** Muḥammad ibn Saʿd al-Baṣrī (168–230 AH / 784–845 CE)
**Death Date:** 230 AH / 845 CE
**Publisher (Arabic):** Dār Ṣādir, Beirut (8 volumes); Dār al-Kutub al-ʿIlmiyyah, Beirut
**Publisher (English):** S. Moinul Haq & H.K. Ghazanfar (Pakistan Historical Society) — partial translation
**Edition:** Arabic: Dār Ṣādir edition; English: Pakistan Historical Society
**Publication Year:** Arabic: 1957 (Dār Ṣādir); English: 1967–1972 (partial)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Arabic at al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; English partial at archive.org
**Physical Copy?:** Arabic editions available at specialist Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online Arabic: al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; Volume 1 covers the biography of the Prophet ﷺ
**Tier:** 2
**Reliability:** The most detailed classical biographical dictionary. Ibn Saʿd was a student of al-Wāqidī and compiled biographical entries for the Prophet ﷺ and thousands of Companions. His biographical data (dates, lineages, children, marriages) is generally the most detailed available and is the primary reference for Profile entries.
**Primary Usage:** Profile entries (Profiles tab in BookDetail) — lineage, dates, roles for all major figures. Biography of Khadījah (most detailed available). Ḥalīmah al-Saʿdiyyah details. ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib's biography.
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-referencing Ibn Hishām's narrative accounts with biographical detail.
**When NOT to Use:** Do not use al-Wāqidī (Ibn Saʿd's primary informant) without noting that al-Wāqidī is considered weak (ḍaʿīf) by major ḥadīth critics (Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal, al-Bukhārī). When Ibn Saʿd traces a narration to al-Wāqidī, treat it as a secondary corroboration only.
**Lessons Cited:** L3, L4, L6, L7, L8, L9, L10 (biographical data)
**Key Passages / Page References (Arabic Dār Ṣādir edition):**
- Vol. 1, pp. 1–100 — Biography of the Prophet ﷺ (birth, lineage, childhood, marriages)
- Vol. 8, pp. 9–26 — Biography of Khadījah bint Khuwaylid (most detailed available)
- Vol. 1, pp. 75–83 — Ḥalīmah al-Saʿdiyyah and the nursing period
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:** None unique to Ibn Saʿd currently in the lessons; his data is incorporated into narrative via Ibn Hishām.
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Ibn Saʿd's biographies of the early Companions who will appear in L11+ (Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, ʿAlī, etc.) are essential references for the Companion series (Volume II).
- His entry on Waraqah ibn Nawfal is directly relevant to L11 (First Revelation).

### T2-004 — AL-BAYHAQĪ — DALĀIL AL-NUBUWWAH

**Arabic Title:** دلائل النبوة ومعرفة أحوال صاحب الشريعة
**English Title:** Proofs of Prophethood and Knowledge of the Conditions of the Possessor of the Law (Dalāʾil al-Nubuwwah)
**Author:** Aḥmad ibn al-Ḥusayn al-Bayhaqī (384–458 AH / 994–1066 CE)
**Death Date:** 458 AH / 1066 CE
**Publisher (Arabic):** Dār al-Kutub al-ʿIlmiyyah, Beirut (7 volumes)
**Publisher (English):** No complete English translation available
**Edition:** Dār al-Kutub al-ʿIlmiyyah, Beirut, 1985
**Publication Year:** Arabic: 1985 (Dār al-Kutub edition)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Arabic at al-Maktaba al-Shāmila
**Physical Copy?:** Arabic available at specialist Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online Arabic: al-Maktaba al-Shāmila
**Tier:** 2 (with caution — see Reliability note below)
**Reliability:** Al-Bayhaqī is a highly respected Shāfiʿī scholar and muḥaddith who compiled this work specifically to gather reports about the signs and proofs of prophethood. He transmits with isnāds and evaluates many narrations, but the subject matter (prophetic signs) means the work includes accounts that cannot be authenticated to T1 level. Used as T2 for broad accounts corroborated by Ibn Hishām; used as T3 for accounts unique to al-Bayhaqī.
**Primary Usage:** The cloud over the Prophet ﷺ on the trade journey (Shām journey, L8); additional details of the signs observed by Maysarah (L10).
**Secondary Usage:** Cross-reference for accounts in Ibn Hishām related to prophetic signs.
**When NOT to Use:** Do not use al-Bayhaqī as a standalone primary source for any account not corroborated by Ibn Hishām or the two Ṣaḥīḥayn. Treat accounts found ONLY in al-Bayhaqī as T3 material.
**Lessons Cited:** L8 (Baḥīrā and Shām journey signs), L10 (Maysarah's report)
**Key Passages / Page References:**
- Vol. 2, pp. 14–25 — Signs associated with the Shām trade journey
- Vol. 2, pp. 60–75 — The signs observed by Maysarah
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:** None unique to al-Bayhaqī in current lessons.
**Future Research Ideas:**
- Al-Bayhaqī's account of the First Revelation and the first days after (Vol. 2, pp. 145–200) is supplementary to Bukhārī 3 for L11 research.

### T3-001 — AL-RAḤĪQ AL-MAKHTŪM

**Arabic Title:** الرحيق المختوم
**English Title:** The Sealed Nectar (Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm)
**Author:** Ṣafī al-Raḥmān al-Mubārakpūrī (1942–2006 CE)
**Death Date:** 2006 CE (contemporary)
**Publisher:** Darussalam (Arabic and English)
**Edition:** Multiple; Darussalam is the standard publisher
**Publication Year:** First published 1979; multiple Darussalam reprints; latest edition 2011 or later
**PDF Available?:** Yes — widely available online; Darussalam sells legitimate PDF
**Physical Copy?:** Yes — widely available at all Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Widely available; most Islamic household libraries
**Tier:** 3
**Reliability:** A synthesis work based primarily on Ibn Hishām and al-Ṭabarī. Awarded first prize at the World Muslim League Sīrah writing competition (1979). Widely used as an accessible overview of the Sīrah. However, it does not always clearly distinguish between strong and weak narrations, and it is a late (20th-century) synthesis — not a classical primary source. It follows classical sources but is itself not a classical source.
**Primary Usage:** Orientation reading when beginning research on a new lesson — to understand the broad narrative structure before diving into T1/T2 sources.
**Secondary Usage:** Identifying which T2 sources discuss a specific event (al-Raḥīq often cites Ibn Hishām by page).
**When NOT to Use:** Never cite al-Raḥīq as a source in the Reader, Companion, or Research Archive. Never include a fact sourced only from al-Raḥīq without verifying it in Ibn Hishām or another T2 source.
**Lessons Cited:** Used as orientation for all lessons; never directly cited in lesson content
**Key Passages / Page References:**
- pp. 41–80 — Pre-Islamic Arabia and childhood (L2–L7 orientation)
- pp. 80–110 — Youth, journeys, marriage (L8–L10 orientation)
- pp. 110–125 — Years before Revelation (L10, L11 orientation)
**Arabic Quotations Preserved:** N/A — T3 source; not directly quoted in lessons.
**Future Research Ideas:**
- When beginning research for L11–L20, use al-Raḥīq pp. 110–200 as the orientation reading before turning to T1/T2 sources.

### T3-002 — IBN KATHĪR — AL-BIDĀYAH WAL-NIHĀYAH

**Arabic Title:** البداية والنهاية
**English Title:** The Beginning and the End (Al-Bidāyah wal-Nihāyah)
**Author:** Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUmar ibn Kathīr al-Dimashqī (701–774 AH / 1301–1373 CE)
**Death Date:** 774 AH / 1373 CE
**Publisher (Arabic):** Dār Iḥyāʾ al-Turāth al-ʿArabī, Beirut (14 volumes)
**Publisher (English):** Darussalam (partial — selected volumes)
**Edition:** Dār Iḥyāʾ edition; Darussalam English edition
**Publication Year:** Arabic: 1988 (Dār Iḥyāʾ); English: 2000 onwards (Darussalam)
**PDF Available?:** Yes — Arabic at al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; some English volumes at Darussalam
**Physical Copy?:** Available at specialist Islamic bookstores
**Shelf Location:** Online Arabic: al-Maktaba al-Shāmila; Volumes 2–3 cover the Sīrah
**Tier:** 3
**Reliability:** A comprehensive Islamic history by the renowned muḥaddith and Qur'ān commentator Ibn Kathīr. He applies ḥadīth criticism to the Sīrah narrations more explicitly than al-Ṭabarī. However, as a later synthesis work (8th century AH), it is treated as T3 for this project. His direct ḥadīth judgments (ṣaḥīḥ, ḍaʿīf, etc.) on specific narrations are sometimes useful as a secondary assessment.
**Primary Usage:** Consultation when Ibn Hishām and al-Ṭabarī are ambiguous about a narration's reliability — Ibn Kathīr often provides an explicit assessment.
**Secondary Usage:** Additional context for events already documented from T1/T2 sources.

## SOURCES EXPLICITLY PROHIBITED

The following categories of sources are prohibited from use in OUR LEGACY under any circumstances:

1. **Mawḍūʿ (fabricated) ḥadīth** — Any narration known to be fabricated. If there is uncertainty about a narration's status, treat it as ḍaʿīf (weak) at minimum and do not use it.

2. **Unverified online citations** — Any "ḥadīth" or "Sīrah quote" encountered on social media, WhatsApp, or popular Islamic websites without a verifiable classical source.

3. **Isrāʾīliyyāt without restriction** — Stories from Jewish or Christian traditions incorporated into Sīrah literature without chain verification. These may appear in al-Ṭabarī; they require explicit flagging and are never presented as Islamic historical fact.

4. **Unattributed popular accounts** — Historical "facts" about the Prophet's ﷺ life that appear in popular books without traceable classical sourcing.

## RESEARCH PRIORITY ORDER

When beginning research for any new lesson, consult sources in this order:

1. **Qur'ān** (T1) — Does the Qur'ān directly address any event in this lesson?
2. **Bukhārī** (T1) — Does Bukhārī contain an authenticated narration about any event or figure in this lesson?
3. **Muslim** (T1) — Same check for Muslim.
4. **Ibn Hishām** (T2) — The primary Sīrah narrative for the event.
5. **Ibn Saʿd** (T2) — Biographical detail for any named person.
6. **Al-Ṭabarī** (T2) — Cross-reference and supplementary narrative.
7. **Al-Bayhaqī** (T2/caution) — Signs and proofs of prophethood narratives only.
8. **Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm** (T3) — Orientation only; trace every claim back to T1/T2 before use.
9. **Ibn Kathīr** (T3) — Assessment of contested narrations.
