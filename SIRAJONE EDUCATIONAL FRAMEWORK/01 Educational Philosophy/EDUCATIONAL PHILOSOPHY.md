## THE CORE BELIEF

Islamic knowledge has always been transmitted person-to-person, generation-to-generation. The masjid, the madrasah, the family circle, the scholar's home — these are the original educational environments of the Ummah.

SirajOne does not try to replace those environments. It tries to support them — to give families, classrooms, and individuals the material, structure, and depth they need to have real educational conversations about the most important lives in history.

The technology is a delivery mechanism. The education happens between people.

---

## WHO THE SERIES IS WRITTEN FOR

The OUR LEGACY series is written for five simultaneous audiences:

### 1. The Independent Adult Reader
A Muslim adult reading alone — on a phone, during lunch, before Fajr, between commitments. They want substance. They can handle scholarly nuance. They are tired of shallow content that skims the surface of the Sīrah and presents it as if there is nothing more to say.

### 2. The Family in Ta'līm
A family sitting together after ʿIshāʾ or on a weekend morning, reading through a lesson as a group. The parent reading aloud, the children listening, the session ending with family discussion. The Companion is the tool that structures this experience. The "~12 min/session" timing is calibrated for this format.

### 3. The Classroom or Study Circle
A teacher, an Imam, a youth group leader — using the lesson as a structured curriculum. The Companion becomes a teaching aid. The Quick Review is a class quiz. The Family Discussion is a group discussion. The Explore Further section guides deeper research.

### 4. The Madrasah Student
A student in a supplementary Islamic education program. The content uses proper Islamic terminology with English explanation, building both English literacy and Islamic knowledge simultaneously. The structure mirrors academic learning: introduction, content, reflection, review.

### 5. The Curious Non-Muslim
Someone who wants to understand the life of the Prophet ﷺ from authentic sources — not from polemics or secondhand summaries. The series is written in a way that assumes no prior knowledge, explains every term, and presents the material with respect and clarity.

## THE SESSION FORMAT

Every lesson is designed for a single reading session of approximately 10–12 minutes per part.

- **Part 1:** 10 minutes of reading
- **Part 2:** 10 minutes of reading
- **Part 3:** 10 minutes of reading
- **Part 4:** 10 minutes of reading (some lessons have 3 or 4 segments)
- **Reflection page:** Discussion questions to close the reading
- **Companion:** ~20 additional minutes for families or study circles

Total per-lesson engagement: 40–60 minutes.

This is intentional. A family can realistically dedicate 45 minutes to Islamic education once or twice a week. The series is designed to fit that reality.

Every lesson teaches not just history but character. Each segment is anchored to a specific Islamic virtue:

- The virtue is named in English and Arabic.
- A Qur'ānic verse or authenticated ḥadīth anchors the virtue.

## WHAT OUR LEGACY IS NOT

### Not a textbook
Textbooks prioritise completeness and accuracy above engagement. OUR LEGACY prioritises engagement and accuracy equally. The writing must hold attention.

### Not a children's book
The series is pitched at adolescents and adults. Children can follow with parental guidance, but the vocabulary, concepts, and questions are not simplified for a young child.

### Not a pop-Islamic summary
The series does not avoid complexity, skip scholarly disagreement, or reduce the Sīrah to a list of famous facts. It engages with real historical questions.

### Not a translation of an Arabic work
The series is original English content grounded in Arabic sources. It is not a simplified paraphrase of al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm or any other single source.

### Not polemical
The series does not defend Islam against attacks, engage with orientalist critiques, or argue with other traditions. It simply teaches what is known and how it is known.

## THE FAMILY DISCUSSION MODEL

Every lesson ends with a Family Discussion section in the Companion. This section is designed on the following model:

1. **Open with a personal question** — connecting the historical event to the reader's own experience.
2. **Move to a community/relationship question** — connecting the virtue to how we treat others.
3. **Go deeper with a theological question** — connecting the event to Allah's wisdom and plan.
4. **Close with an application question** — what will the reader do differently because of this lesson?

Each discussion question includes a **Facilitator Note** in italics — a guide for the parent, teacher, or discussion leader that explains what the question is trying to draw out, and suggests how to guide the conversation if it stalls.

This model was developed specifically because Islamic family education often suffers from one of two problems:
- The question is too vague ("what did you learn from this lesson?")
- The expected answer is too rigid (there is a "right" answer and no real discussion)

The OUR LEGACY discussion model produces genuine reflection by asking questions that have no single correct answer.

## THE EDUCATIONAL PROGRESSION WITHIN THE SĪRAH SERIES

The series is not a biography. It is a structured educational journey through the major events and character of the Prophet ﷺ. Each lesson is chosen because:

1. It represents a significant moment in the Sīrah.
2. It carries a virtue lesson that applies to everyday Muslim life.
3. It can sustain 40–50 minutes of educational engagement.
4. It connects naturally to the lesson before and after it.

**Not every event in the Sīrah becomes a lesson.** Minor events are referenced in Reader text or in Companion sections but do not receive their own lesson unless they meet the criteria above.

## THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN READER AND COMPANION

The Reader (EbookReader) and the Companion (LessonGuide) are two halves of a single educational experience.

**Reader** = narrative and history  
**Companion** = reflection and application

The Reader gives the content. The Companion gives the tools to engage with it.

A family who reads only the Reader has learned facts. A family who uses both the Reader and the Companion has had an educational experience.

The Companion is placed before the Closing page — appearing on the Reflection page (penultimate page) — because the ideal flow is:

1. Read the lesson (Segments 1–4).
2. Reach the Reflection page.
3. Open the Companion to deepen the engagement.
4. Return to read the Closing page as a conclusion.

## ON LANGUAGE AND TONE

The series is written in:
- Standard English (not British, not American, not regional)
- Clear, dignified prose — never casual, never academic
- Sentences that can be read aloud comfortably
- Paragraphs that breathe — not too dense, not too short

The tone is:
- Respectful toward the subject
- Honest with the reader
- Warm but not sentimental
- Precise but not dry

The model is: write the way a deeply knowledgeable teacher would speak to a group of intelligent, sincere adults who deserve the full truth.

## WHY THE SERIES BEGAN WITH THE SĪRAH

The Sīrah of the Prophet ﷺ is the most important narrative in Islamic education. Before the Companions, before the scholars, before any other personality — the life of the Prophet ﷺ is the foundation. Every other volume in the OUR LEGACY series will reference the Sīrah because every other personality's life is shaped by their relationship to the Prophet ﷺ.

Starting with the Sīrah also establishes all the systems — writing standards, source hierarchy, Companion framework, deployment workflow — that every subsequent volume will use.

The Sīrah is Volume I. When it is complete, it will contain approximately 30 lessons covering the Prophet's life from before his birth to his legacy after his death.
