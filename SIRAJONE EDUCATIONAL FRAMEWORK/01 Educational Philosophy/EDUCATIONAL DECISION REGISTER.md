## PURPOSE

This register documents the major structural decisions about how OUR LEGACY is designed as an educational experience. It is distinct from the Editorial Decisions Log (09), which records content-specific choices. This register records the deeper educational architecture decisions — why the series is built the way it is.

Every decision here was made for a reason. If you are tempted to change any of these structures, read the rationale and consequences sections first.

---

## FORMAT

Each entry contains:
- **Decision** — what was decided
- **Reason** — why this decision was made
- **Educational benefit** — what learners gain from this design
- **Consequences if changed** — what would break or suffer if this decision were reversed

---

## EDR-001 — EVERY LESSON HAS FOUR TEACHING SEGMENTS

**Decision:** Each lesson is divided into 3 or 4 named segments (SegmentPages), each anchored to a specific Islamic virtue and Qur'anic verse or authenticated hadith. The standard is 4 segments; 3 segments are permitted when the historical content genuinely does not sustain a fourth.

**Reason:** A single long reading without structural breaks does not support active learning. Each segment functions as a mini-lesson within the larger lesson. The virtue anchor at the top of each segment signals to the reader: "This section is teaching you something specific." Without that signal, the reader processes narrative but may not retain the lesson.

**Educational benefit:** The reader can read one segment in approximately 10 minutes. A family that cannot complete a full lesson can complete one segment, still receive a complete virtue lesson with its Qur'anic anchor, and resume from the next segment the following session. Each segment is educationally self-contained while contributing to the larger lesson.

**Consequences if changed:** Removing the segment structure makes the lesson a single long narrative. The virtue framework loses its anchor points. The reading becomes less structured for classroom and family use. Individual segment completion (as a standalone session) becomes impossible.

## EDR-002 — THE VIRTUE FRAMEWORK IS THE EDUCATIONAL SPINE

**Decision:** Every segment is anchored to a named Islamic virtue (in English and Arabic), illustrated by the narrative of that segment, and supported by a Qur'anic verse or authenticated hadith.

**Reason:** History without application is trivia. The virtue framework ensures that every historical event the reader encounters is connected to a quality of character — something the reader can aspire to embody. Families remember the virtue long after they forget the date or the name.

**Educational benefit:** The reader learns two things simultaneously: the historical event and the virtue it illustrates. The reflection questions and the Companion then connect that virtue to the reader's own life. This three-stage movement (history → virtue → reflection) produces genuine character education, not just historical knowledge.

**Consequences if changed:** Removing the virtue anchors reduces the series to historical narration. The app becomes a biography reader rather than a character education tool. The Family Discussion section of the Companion loses its coherence. The series loses its primary educational identity.

## EDR-003 — THE REFLECTION PAGE COMES BEFORE THE COMPANION CTA

**Decision:** The Companion CTA appears on the ReflectionPage (penultimate page), not on the ClosingPage (final page). The reading flow is: Segments → Reflection Page (with Companion CTA) → Closing Page.

**Reason:** The Companion is designed to deepen reflection, not to follow conclusion. If the CTA appears on the ClosingPage, the family reads the lesson, receives the conclusion, and then is offered deeper engagement — but the emotional and intellectual moment has already passed. By placing the CTA on the ReflectionPage, the Companion supplements the reflective mode the reader is already in.

**Educational benefit:** The ideal session flow is: read all segments → reach the Reflection page → open the Companion → use the Family Discussion → return to the Closing page as a conclusion. The Closing page then functions as a genuine ending, not an advertisement for more content.

**Consequences if changed:** Moving the CTA to the ClosingPage reduces the Companion uptake because the reader has already mentally concluded the lesson. Moving it to an earlier page (mid-lesson) would interrupt the reading flow. The current placement is the result of implementation testing (see Editorial Decision ED-004 in 09 — EDITORIAL DECISIONS LOG).

## EDR-004 — THE FAMILY DISCUSSION FOLLOWS A FOUR-QUESTION ARC

**Decision:** Every Family Discussion section in the Companion contains exactly 4 questions, following this fixed arc: Q1 (personal/experiential) → Q2 (relational/community) → Q3 (theological/deeper) → Q4 (application).

**Reason:** Family and classroom discussions fail most often for one of two reasons: the question is too abstract to connect with, or the question has a predetermined "right" answer that shuts down conversation. The four-question arc is specifically designed to avoid both failure modes.

Q1 grounds the discussion in personal experience — everyone has an answer. Q2 extends that experience to relationships with others. Q3 raises the level of the discussion to Islamic theology and wisdom. Q4 closes with action — something the reader will actually do differently. This progression mirrors the learning stages in Bloom's Taxonomy (remember → understand → apply → evaluate) while remaining accessible to non-academic audiences.

**Educational benefit:** A family using this structure regularly will develop a consistent habit of Islamic reflection. The facilitator notes in each discussion question provide specific guidance so that a parent without teaching training can lead a productive session. The arc ensures the discussion moves forward rather than cycling on the same point.

**Consequences if changed:** Adding a fifth question usually dilutes the arc — one of the four purposes is duplicated. Removing a question usually leaves a gap (no grounding, no theology, no action). Changing the order disrupts the progressive movement from personal to theological. The arc should be preserved exactly.

## EDR-005 — THE QURʾĀN / ṢAḤĪḤ ḤADĪTH / SĪRAH SOURCES ARE DISTINGUISHED

**Decision:** The source hierarchy (Tier 1/2/3) is not merely a citation system. It is an educational system. Readers are explicitly told, through the hedging language of the text, what level of confidence to place in each claim.

**Reason:** Islamic epistemology has always distinguished between the certain (qatʿī) and the probable (ẓannī). Presenting all historical claims with the same level of confidence — whether from the Qur'an or from a single weak narration in al-Ṭabarī — is not just academically dishonest. It is educationally harmful. It trains readers to accept all claims equally, which produces brittle faith (vulnerable to correction) rather than grounded knowledge.

**Educational benefit:** A reader who encounters a discrepancy between this series and another source (e.g., a different age given for Khadījah) is not confused — they have been taught that this is a legitimate scholarly disagreement. They know where to look (the classical sources) and how to evaluate the evidence. The series teaches research methodology as well as content.

**Consequences if changed:** Collapsing the tier distinction and presenting all claims with equal confidence would make the series faster to write but would misrepresent the actual epistemic status of the content. This would be a violation of Master Principle 2 (Intellectual Honesty) and would undermine the series' credibility with knowledgeable readers.

## EDR-006 — HISTORICAL CONTINUITY IS PROTECTED ACROSS LESSONS

**Decision:** Each lesson may only narrate events up to its designated stopping point. Future events are anticipated (in the nextLessonPreview) but never narrated. This applies especially to the First Revelation, which is reserved entirely for the First Revelation lesson (L11) regardless of how closely the preceding lesson approaches it.

**Reason:** The weight of a historical event is diminished when it is embedded in a lesson about something else. The First Revelation is the most important moment in the narrative. It should receive the full attention of a dedicated lesson, not a paragraph within a lesson about the years before.

More practically: if events are narrated out of order or in two places, inconsistencies become very difficult to manage. The reader encounters the same event twice with potentially different details.

**Educational benefit:** Each lesson creates its own complete arc of tension and resolution. When Lesson 10 ends "in the years of quiet before the dawn — before everything changed," the reader's anticipation is genuine. The First Revelation lesson then delivers the event at full weight. This is a narrative and pedagogical structure, not just a stylistic preference.

**Consequences if changed:** If the First Revelation is partially narrated in Lesson 10, Lesson 11 becomes redundant. The emotional and educational arc collapses. This principle applies to every lesson boundary — each lesson should end where it ends for a reason.

## EDR-007 — EVERY CLOSING PAGE HAS A DISCOVERY NOTE

**Decision:** Every ClosingPage contains a `discoveryNote` — a brief pointer to related content in the Profiles, Maps, or References tabs in BookDetail.

**Reason:** The Reader and the reference tabs (BookDetail) are separate experiences. Most readers who finish a lesson do not intuitively open the BookDetail tabs to explore. The discoveryNote bridges the two experiences — it tells the reader that there is more to explore and gives a specific reason to explore it.

**Educational benefit:** A reader who follows the discoveryNote encounters the map of the Ḥijāz, or the profile of Khadījah, or the reference card for Sīrat Ibn Hishām. This extends the educational engagement beyond the Reader without requiring a separate prompt. It reinforces the lesson content in a different mode (visual/encyclopedic vs. narrative).

**Consequences if changed:** Removing discoveryNotes makes the BookDetail tabs invisible to most readers. The supplementary educational content becomes unreachable in practice. The investment in building Profiles, Maps, and References tabs is lost.

**Writing rule for discoveryNotes:** See **03 — LESSON ARCHITECTURE** (Discovery Note System section).

## EDR-008 — THE COMPANION EXISTS AS A SEPARATE SCREEN, NOT AN OVERLAY

**Decision:** The Lesson Companion opens as a completely separate screen (LessonGuide.tsx), not as a modal, drawer, or overlay within the Reader.

**Reason:** The Companion is a substantial educational tool — 9 sections covering multiple dimensions of the lesson. Embedding it in an overlay creates a cramped experience and implies that the Companion is secondary to the Reader. A dedicated screen signals to the reader that the Companion deserves its own focused engagement.

**Educational benefit:** The separation creates a clear distinction between modes of engagement: reading mode (EbookReader) and learning mode (LessonGuide). Families who open the Companion are intentionally shifting from reading to reflection. The mode change supports this intention.

**Consequences if changed:** An inline Companion (modal or drawer) would technically work but would reduce engagement with the deeper sections. The Family Discussion and Further Reading sections especially benefit from full-screen space.

## EDR-009 — LESSONS ARE NUMBERED BY PART, NOT BY SEQUENCE

**Decision:** The PartDividerPage subtitle is "Part N" rather than "Lesson N." The Companion key is `sirah_journey:N`. The chapter titles are narrative (e.g., "Year of the Elephant") rather than "Lesson 3."

**Reason:** In an educational series delivered as a reading experience, "Part N" positions each lesson as a chapter in a larger narrative rather than a classroom lesson. This is a deliberate tonal choice: the series is not a curriculum packet — it is a continuous journey. The word "Part" carries the connotation of a serialized story; "Lesson" carries the connotation of a classroom exercise.

**Educational benefit:** The "Part" framing encourages completion motivation: readers want to know what comes next. They are not doing homework; they are reading a story with an educational framework layered on top.

**Consequences if changed:** Changing "Part N" to "Lesson N" in all PartDividerPages would require updating the Companion lookup key in EbookReader (`sirah_journey:N` is derived from the part number). This would be a breaking change requiring updates to all 10 existing Companions and all deployed content. Not worth doing for a tonal preference.

## EDR-010 — THE MEMORY GEM IS FROM TIER 1 WHERE POSSIBLE

**Decision:** Every Companion's Memory Gem (the single quotation families carry with them from the lesson) must come from a Tier 1 source (Qur'an, Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, or Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim) where the lesson content makes this possible.

**Reason:** The Memory Gem is the one thing families are most likely to retain and recite after the lesson. If it comes from a weak or disputed source, families may recite an inauthentic statement as though it were established. The Tier 1 requirement ensures that the most remembered element of every lesson is the most authenticated.

**Educational benefit:** Over the course of 30 lessons, families who complete the Sīrah series will have memorised 30 Tier 1 quotations. This is a significant supplement to Islamic knowledge built through the incidental retention of the Memory Gem across sessions.

**Consequences if changed:** Allowing Tier 2 Memory Gems for convenience reduces the standard. It should only happen when no Tier 1 source captures the essence of the lesson — and even then, the Memory Gem must be explicitly attributed as Tier 2.

## EDR-011 — THE SERIES DOES NOT SIMPLIFY FOR CHILDREN

**Decision:** The vocabulary, sentence structure, and conceptual depth of the Reader are pitched at adolescents and adults. Children can participate with parental guidance, but the content is not adjusted downward for a young-child audience.

**Reason:** The most damaging thing that can happen to Islamic education at the family level is adults being embarrassed by the simplicity of the material. If the content is written at a children's level, adult family members disengage. If adults disengage from family Ta'lim, the practice collapses. The material must sustain adult engagement for the family model to work.

**Educational benefit:** Children who are read to at a higher level than they fully comprehend develop their language, vocabulary, and conceptual range. The virtue framework and storytelling structure make the content accessible even when individual words are new. The Family Discussion brings the content down to a level children can engage with.

**Consequences if changed:** Writing at a children's level would make the series accessible to primary school children reading independently but would lose the adult and family audience. The series has many competitors at the children's level. It has very few at the adult-family level.

## EDR-012 — THE REFLECTION PAGE HAS 4-5 QUESTIONS, NOT MORE

**Decision:** The ReflectionPage contains 4–5 reflection questions. No more.

**Reason:** A list of 8–10 reflection questions signals to the reader that thorough reflection requires 8–10 answers. This is overwhelming and usually results in none being answered. Four to five questions create a realistic and achievable reflection task.

**Educational benefit:** Each question is designed to be a genuine entry point for discussion. Fewer, better questions produce deeper engagement than many shallow questions. The Family Discussion in the Companion then provides the additional depth for families who want to go further.

**Consequences if changed:** More than 5 questions makes the Reflection page feel like an exam. Fewer than 4 may leave important angles of the lesson unexplored. The 4–5 range has been consistent across all 10 lessons and should be maintained.

## EDR-013 — SCHOLARLY DISAGREEMENT IS PRESENTED HONESTLY, NOT RESOLVED ARTIFICIALLY

**Decision:** When classical scholars disagree on a historical detail, the disagreement is presented to the reader honestly. The dominant position is followed, with the alternative position attributed and acknowledged. The reader is never given a false sense of certainty about disputed matters.

**Reason:** Islamic scholarship has always operated with legitimate difference of opinion (ikhtilāf). Concealing disagreement from readers produces a brittle understanding that collapses when they encounter the alternative position elsewhere. Honest presentation of scholarly disagreement models intellectual humility.

**Educational benefit:** Readers learn to hold historical details with appropriate confidence — certain about what is certain, open about what is disputed. This produces epistemically mature Muslims who can engage with scholarly nuance rather than feeling threatened by it.

**Consequences if changed:** Presenting the majority position without acknowledging alternatives is technically simpler to write but epistemically dishonest. When readers later discover the alternative position (e.g., Khadījah's age), they may distrust the series for having concealed it.

## EDR-014 — THE SERIES BEGINS WITH THE SĪRAH, NOT THE COMPANIONS

**Decision:** Volume I of OUR LEGACY is the Sīrah of the Prophet ﷺ. The Companions, Mothers of the Believers, and scholars come in later volumes.

**Reason:** Every subsequent personality in Islamic history is defined by their relationship to the Prophet ﷺ. Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, Khadījah, ʿĀʾishah, Ibn Masʿūd — their greatness is inseparable from their connection to the Prophet ﷺ. Beginning with the Sīrah establishes the central reference point against which every subsequent life is measured.

Practically: beginning with the Sīrah also establishes all the systems (writing standards, source hierarchy, Companion framework, deployment workflow) that every subsequent volume will inherit. The Sīrah is the proving ground for the entire framework.

**Educational benefit:** Readers who complete Volume I have a full understanding of the prophetic model before they study those who followed it. This gives every subsequent volume its proper context.

**Consequences if changed:** Beginning with a Companion or a Caliph without first establishing the prophetic framework would require those volumes to do double work — establishing the Prophet's ﷺ character through the lens of the Companion rather than directly.
