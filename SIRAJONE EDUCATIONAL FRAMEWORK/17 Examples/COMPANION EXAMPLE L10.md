## THE L10 COMPANION KEY FACTS (EXAMPLE)

**Why this section is highlighted:** The age of Khadījah at marriage is a real scholarly disagreement. This Key Facts section shows how the OUR LEGACY standard handles disputed details — presenting the dominant position clearly while honestly acknowledging alternatives.

```
keyFacts: [
  {
    label: "Age of the Prophet ﷺ at the time of the trade journey",
    detail: "Twenty-five years old, according to the classical Sīrah — Sīrat Ibn Hishām drawing on Ibn Isḥāq.",
    note: "This is the widely accepted position across classical scholarship.",
  },
  {
    label: "Age of Khadījah at the time of marriage",
    detail: "The most widely transmitted classical position, recorded in Ibn Hishām, is forty years old. Some narrations recorded by al-Ṭabarī and others suggest she may have been younger — 28 or 25 years old at the time.",
    note: "The classical position of forty is followed by the majority of scholars and is the position adopted in this lesson. Whatever her age, her wisdom, independence, and character are undisputed across all accounts.",
  },
  {
    label: "Who proposed the marriage?",
    detail: "Khadījah ؓ proposed to Muḥammad ﷺ. She sent her trusted friend Nafīsah bint Munabbih to ask Muḥammad ﷺ whether he would consider marriage. This is recorded clearly in the Sīrat Ibn Hishām.",
  },
  {
    label: "Primary source for this lesson",
    detail: "Sīrat Ibn Hishām, drawing on the original Sīrah of Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq — the earliest and most comprehensive biography of the Prophet ﷺ.",
  },
  {
    label: "The Memory Gem ḥadīth — source and number",
    detail: "Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ḥadīth 3816. This is the Prophet ﷺ's own statement about Khadījah ؓ, narrated after her death.",
    note: "Note: Bukhārī 3816 is NOT the same as Bukhārī 3. Bukhārī 3 is ʿĀʾishah's narration of the First Revelation — a completely separate ḥadīth.",
  },
],
```

## THE L10 MEMORY GEM (EXAMPLE)

This is the correct Memory Gem for L10 — showing the format with attribution and exact citation:

```
memoryGem: `"She believed in me when people disbelieved me. 
She assisted me with her wealth when people deprived me. 
And Allah blessed me with children through her."

— The Prophet ﷺ about Khadījah bint Khuwaylid ؓ
Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ḥadīth 3816`,
```

**Note:** This quote is from AFTER Khadījah's death. The Prophet ﷺ spoke these words years later when someone questioned his continuing reverence for her. ʿĀʾishah raḍiyallāhu ʿanhā narrated this exchange.

## THE L10 FAMILY DISCUSSION Q1 (EXAMPLE)

This shows how a Q1 (personal/experiential) question is written with its facilitatorNote:

```
{
  question: "Think of someone in your life who believed in you before you believed in yourself. 
  What did that support mean to you? How did it change what you were able to do?",
  
  facilitatorNote: `This question opens the discussion by grounding the lesson in personal experience. 
  Khadijah's support for the Prophet ﷺ is one of the most significant examples of belief-before-proof 
  in history. By asking participants to recall their own experience of being believed in, you help them 
  feel the weight of what she gave him. 
  
  If the discussion stalls, prompt: 'What would have been harder for the Prophet ﷺ without Khadijah's 
  support during those first terrifying moments after the Revelation?' This brings it back to the lesson 
  while keeping the personal connection active.`,
},
```

## THE L10 ONE-MINUTE SUMMARY (EXAMPLE)

This shows the correct length, scope, and what to exclude:

```
oneMinuteSummary: `Lesson Ten traces the life of Muḥammad ﷺ between the ages of twenty-five and forty — 
the years immediately preceding the first Revelation. A respected but modest young man in Mecca, 
he undertook a trade journey to Syria on behalf of the wealthy merchant Khadījah bint Khuwaylid ؓ. 
His character during that journey — his honesty, his dignity, his reliability — so impressed 
Khadījah's trusted companion Maysarah that she sought to marry him. Khadījah sent her friend 
Nafīsah as an intermediary. Muḥammad ﷺ agreed, and the two were married.

The lesson follows their fifteen years of marriage: a home built on character, trust, and loyalty. 
Khadījah ؓ was the first person to believe in him — and that belief, as the Prophet ﷺ himself 
testified in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, was one of the greatest gifts of his life. The lesson ends in 
the years of quiet before the dawn — before everything changed.`,
```

**What is correctly excluded:** Any narration of the First Revelation. The summary ends at "before everything changed" — anticipation only.
