## CORE QUESTION THIS LESSON ANSWERS

What were the events surrounding the birth of the Prophet ﷺ? What was the Year of the Elephant, why does it matter, and what do the classical sources tell us — and not tell us — about the birth itself?

---

## KEY SOURCES CONSULTED

- Sīrat Ibn Hishām — the account of Abraha's expedition and the Year of the Elephant; birth narrative (Tier 2)
- Qur'ān — Sūrat al-Fīl (105): the only Tier 1 account of the Year of the Elephant (Tier 1)
- Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī — authenticated narrations relevant to the birth period (Tier 1)
- Al-Ṭabarī — birth narrative supplementary details; the death of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib (Tier 2)
- Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm — synthesis of the birth account (Tier 3, cross-reference only)

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS RAISED DURING WRITING

**1. How much detail to include about the Year of the Elephant?**
Resolved: Sūrat al-Fīl gives the core event (the army of birds carrying clay stones). The lesson focuses on the Qur'ānic account first, then supplements with Ibn Hishām's historical details. The miraculous nature of the event is stated from the Qur'ān (T1); the historical narrative comes from Ibn Hishām (T2). This layering makes the tier system visible to the reader without being academic.

**2. What can be established about the exact birth date?**
Resolved: The widely transmitted report is 12 Rabīʿ al-Awwal, 53 BH (approximately April–May, 570 CE). However, classical scholars differ on the exact date. The lesson acknowledges this without dwelling on the disagreement — the date is mentioned with appropriate hedging. The Year of the Elephant as the birth year is more firmly established than the exact date.

**3. The death of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib — before or after birth?**
Resolved: The dominant position in Ibn Hishām and other classical sources is that ʿAbdullāh died before the Prophet's ﷺ birth, during his trade journey to Madīnah. This position is followed. The alternative (he died after the birth) is acknowledged.

**4. Should the lesson include the reported signs that accompanied the birth?**
Resolved: The lesson includes the major accounts narrated by Ibn Hishām (the light that Āminah saw, the accounts of people who noticed signs). These are narrated with attribution ("it is narrated that...") rather than stated as fact. They are Tier 2 narrations. The lesson does not include fabricated or very weak narrations not found in the classical sources.

**5. What about the father's death and its emotional weight?**
Resolved: ʿAbdullāh's early death is included because it is directly relevant to the prophetic experience of being an orphan (Qur'ān 93:6). The emotional dimension (Āminah as a widow, raising the child alone) is given appropriate weight without sentimentalizing.

## SOURCES ACCEPTED

- The Year of the Elephant: Sūrat al-Fīl (T1), supplemented by Ibn Hishām (T2). The Qur'ānic account is definitive; Ibn Hishām provides historical context.
- Birth date (12 Rabīʿ al-Awwal): transmitted via Sīrat Ibn Hishām and other classical sources (T2). Acknowledged as the dominant position, not stated as universally agreed.
- ʿAbdullāh's death before the birth: dominant position in Ibn Hishām (T2). Followed with acknowledgment of the alternative.
- Āminah bint Wahb as the Prophet's ﷺ mother: unanimously reported across all classical sources.

## EDITORIAL DECISIONS MADE DURING THIS LESSON

- The virtue for this lesson centred on tawakkul (reliance on Allah) — illustrated by Āminah's situation (widowed, pregnant) and the miraculous protection of the Kaʿbah in the Year of the Elephant.
- The lesson does not include accounts found only in later popular sources (Dalāʾil al-Nubuwwah type material) without naming and qualifying them.
- The lesson covers the birth but does not describe the childhood — that begins in L4. Strict stopping point maintained.
