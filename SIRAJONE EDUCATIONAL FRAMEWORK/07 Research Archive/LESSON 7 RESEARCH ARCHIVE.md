## CORE QUESTION THIS LESSON ANSWERS

Who was ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib ibn Hāshim? What was his relationship with the Prophet ﷺ during the two years of his guardianship, what do classical sources tell us about his character, and what happened when he too died?

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS RAISED DURING WRITING

**1. What was ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib's religious status?**
Resolved: ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib died before prophethood and therefore before Islam. Classical sources consistently describe him as holding to a form of Ḥanīfiyyah (pure monotheism in the pre-Islamic tradition) — he did not engage in idol worship. This is significant and is mentioned in the lesson: the Prophet's ﷺ grandfather was not a polytheist. The lesson does not resolve the theological question of his afterlife status — this is a matter of scholarly disagreement outside the scope of this lesson.

**2. What is known of the character of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib?**
Resolved: Ibn Hishām and Ibn Saʿd describe him as a man of exceptional honour and standing in Mecca — the leader of Quraysh, the protector of the Kaʿbah, the man who rediscovered Zamzam, the one who vowed to sacrifice a son (the vow that led to the rescue of ʿAbdullāh). His character as a guardian is described positively: he gave the orphaned child a special place in his household.

**3. The famous account of the child at ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib's seat.**
Resolved: Ibn Hishām narrates that the Prophet ﷺ as a child would sit on ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib's special seat (reserved for the grandfather alone) and that ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib would say: "This son of mine has a great station." This account is narrated as "Ibn Hishām records" — not stated as authenticated ḥadīth.

**4. When did ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib die and how old was the Prophet ﷺ?**
Resolved: Classical sources report that ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib died approximately two years after Āminah — when the Prophet ﷺ was approximately eight years old. The lesson uses this figure with the appropriate hedging.

**5. The transition from ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib to Abū Ṭālib's guardianship.**
Resolved: ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib reportedly specified Abū Ṭālib (full brother of ʿAbdullāh, the Prophet's father) as the new guardian before he died. Ibn Hishām narrates this transition. The lesson ends at this transition point; Abū Ṭālib's guardianship and the journey to Shām begin in L8.

## EDITORIAL DECISIONS MADE DURING THIS LESSON

- Virtue: tawāḍuʿ (humility) and shukr (gratitude) — illustrated through the orphan child's experience of love from his grandfather despite having no father, no mother, no wealth.
- The Qur'ānic verse (93:6–8) frames the entire lesson: "Did He not find you an orphan and give shelter? ... Did He not find you wandering and guided you? ... Did He not find you in need and made you self-sufficient?" The three questions span the three losses — father, mother, grandfather — across the first eight years of the Prophet's ﷺ life.
- The lesson explicitly does not resolve the theological status of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib. This keeps the lesson educationally safe.
