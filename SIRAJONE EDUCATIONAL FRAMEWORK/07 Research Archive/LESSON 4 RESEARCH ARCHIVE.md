## CORE QUESTION THIS LESSON ANSWERS

Why was the Prophet ﷺ sent to the desert as an infant? Who was Ḥalīmah al-Saʿdiyyah, what happened in her care, and what do the classical sources establish about the barakah that accompanied the child?

---

## KEY SOURCES CONSULTED

- Sīrat Ibn Hishām — the account of Ḥalīmah al-Saʿdiyyah, her household, and the blessings that came with the Prophet ﷺ (Tier 2)
- Ibn Saʿd, al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrā — detailed account of the desert nursing period (Tier 2)
- Al-Ṭabarī — supplementary details on the nursing years (Tier 2)
- Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim — the ḥadīth of the opening of the chest (Ḥadīth 162) — this event is partially covered in L4 and fully in L5 (Tier 1)

---

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS RAISED DURING WRITING

**1. Is the practice of wet nursing / desert fostering well-documented for the Quraysh?**
Resolved: Yes. Ibn Hishām and Ibn Saʿd both confirm that it was the established custom of noble Meccan families (particularly Quraysh) to send infants to bedouin wet nurses in the desert. The rationale given in classical sources: cleaner air, stronger physical constitution, pure Arabic language acquisition. The lesson explains this custom so the reader understands that the Prophet ﷺ being sent was not abandonment — it was a mark of care.

**2. How should the barakah in Ḥalīmah's household be narrated?**
Resolved: The accounts of her donkey becoming strong, her dried-up goat giving milk, and her increased provision are reported by Ibn Hishām from Ḥalīmah's own account. These are narrated as "she reported" or "Ibn Hishām narrates from Ḥalīmah" — attributed rather than stated as unconditioned fact. The lesson preserves the spiritual dimension without overstating the source's authority.

**3. Was the opening of the chest (shaqq al-ṣadr) in this period or a later one?**
Resolved: The first opening of the chest is reported by Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim as occurring during the period with Ḥalīmah, when the Prophet ﷺ was approximately four years old. L4 covers the desert period up to and including the first opening of the chest. L5 begins immediately after. The event is not split across two lessons.

**4. How long did the Prophet ﷺ remain with Ḥalīmah?**
Resolved: Classical sources report approximately two to four years. Ibn Saʿd's account is followed: the standard nursing period was two years, but Ḥalīmah kept the child longer. The lesson does not commit to an exact year count beyond "the first years of his life."

**5. Ḥalīmah's return of the child — what were the circumstances?**
Resolved: After the opening of the chest, Ḥalīmah was frightened and returned the Prophet ﷺ to Āminah. Ibn Hishām narrates Ḥalīmah's account of this. The lesson presents her decision with empathy — fear, not cruelty.

## SOURCES ACCEPTED

- Wet nursing custom of the Quraysh: Ibn Hishām (T2), Ibn Saʿd (T2). Consistent across sources.
- Barakah in Ḥalīmah's household: Ḥalīmah's own account as narrated by Ibn Hishām (T2). Narrated with attribution.
- Opening of the chest (first occurrence): Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 162 (T1). This is the strongest source for this event.
- Return to Āminah: Ibn Hishām (T2).

## EDITORIAL DECISIONS MADE DURING THIS LESSON

- Virtue: mercy and gratitude — illustrated by Ḥalīmah's acknowledgment of the barakah that came through this child, and the lesson's broader point about recognizing divine gifts.
- The lesson does not narrate the content of the opening of the chest beyond what Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim establishes. The two angels, the golden basin, the removal of the clot — these are T1 content and stated accordingly.
