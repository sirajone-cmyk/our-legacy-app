## CORE QUESTION THIS LESSON ANSWERS

What was the world like before the Prophet ﷺ was born? What was Arabia — its religion, its social structure, its tribal culture, its geography — and why does understanding it matter for understanding the Sīrah?

- Sīrat Ibn Hishām — introductory sections on Arab tribes, Quraysh lineage, pre-Islamic Mecca (Tier 2)
- Al-Ṭabarī, Tārīkh al-Rusul wal-Mulūk — pre-Islamic Arabia and the Jāhiliyyah period (Tier 2)
- Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm — the world before the Prophet's birth, Arabian geography (Tier 3)
- Qur'ān — references to the Jāhiliyyah, the condition of humanity before revelation (Tier 1)

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS RAISED DURING WRITING

**1. How much pre-Islamic Arabian context is necessary before the birth narrative?**
Resolved: The lesson needed enough context for the reader to understand why the birth of the Prophet ﷺ was significant — the religious vacuum, the tribal structure, the Quraysh's role as custodians of the Kaʿbah. Too much context buries the lesson in history; too little makes the birth seem arbitrary. The standard adopted: establish the religious and social condition of Arabia, identify Mecca and the Quraysh, set the scene for the arrival.

**2. How should the Jāhiliyyah be described without being dismissive of pre-Islamic Arab culture?**
Resolved: The Jāhiliyyah is a religious and moral designation in Islamic scholarship — not a condemnation of all pre-Islamic culture. The lesson acknowledges the virtues the Arabs possessed (hospitality, loyalty, eloquence, courage) while presenting the theological condition (polytheism, tribal violence, the burial of daughters) honestly. The word "Jāhiliyyah" is explained on first use.

**3. Should the lesson name specific pre-Islamic practices (e.g., waʾd al-banāt — female infanticide)?**
Resolved: Yes. The Qur'ān explicitly references these practices (Surah al-Naḥl 16:58–59, Surah al-Takwīr 81:8–9). Not naming them would sanitise the historical context in a way that reduces the reader's understanding of what the Prophet ﷺ came to change.

**4. How detailed should the Quraysh lineage be?**
Resolved: The lineage of the Prophet ﷺ through the Quraysh (ʿAdnānī Arabs, Banū Hāshim, ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib) is included at the level needed to understand future lessons. Full genealogical tables are not included in the Reader — they are reference material appropriate for the Profiles tab.

## SOURCES ACCEPTED

- The general description of Arabia's religious condition before Islam: Sīrat Ibn Hishām (Tier 2), corroborated by multiple Qur'ānic references (Tier 1).
- The Quraysh as the custodians of the Kaʿbah and the leading tribe of Mecca: Sīrat Ibn Hishām (Tier 2), widely accepted across all classical sources.
- The practice of waʾd al-banāt: referenced explicitly in the Qur'ān (T1) and detailed in Sīrat Ibn Hishām (T2).
- The Arab virtue tradition (murūwwah): classical sources, including the Sīrah literature.

---

## EDITORIAL DECISIONS MADE DURING THIS LESSON

- The virtue chosen for this lesson was broad — contextual rather than tied to a single specific event — because the lesson is panoramic (the world, not a single event). This is the only lesson with this characteristic.
- The word "Jāhiliyyah" is used and explained. It is not avoided.
- The lesson deliberately ends by positioning the reader for the birth narrative (L3), not by narrating the birth.
