## CORE QUESTION THIS LESSON ANSWERS

When and how did Āminah bint Wahb die? What were the circumstances of the journey to Madīnah, and how did her death shape the early life of the Prophet ﷺ?

---

## KEY SOURCES CONSULTED

- Sīrat Ibn Hishām — the journey to Yathrib (Madīnah) and Āminah's death at al-Abwāʾ (Tier 2)
- Ibn Saʿd, al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrā — the account of Āminah's death and ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib's guardianship (Tier 2)
- Al-Ṭabarī — supplementary account of the journey and death (Tier 2)
- Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim — the ḥadīth about the Prophet's ﷺ request to visit his mother's grave (Ḥadīth 976): authenticated context for the significance of Āminah's memory (Tier 1)

---

## RESEARCH QUESTIONS RAISED DURING WRITING

**1. Why did Āminah travel to Yathrib?**
Resolved: Ibn Hishām narrates that Āminah took the Prophet ﷺ to Yathrib (Madīnah) to visit the grave of his father ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and to introduce the child to his maternal relatives (the Banū ʿAdī ibn al-Najjār). This purpose is stated in the lesson.

**2. How old was the Prophet ﷺ when Āminah died?**
Resolved: Classical sources report approximately six years of age. Ibn Hishām gives this figure. The lesson uses "approximately six years old" — not stated with false precision.

**3. How should Āminah's death be narrated?**
Resolved: She died at al-Abwāʾ, on the return journey from Yathrib to Mecca. The lesson narrates this with appropriate solemnity but without sentimentality. The focus is on the prophetic experience of loss — the Qur'ānic frame (93:6, "Did He not find you an orphan and give shelter?") establishes that this loss was within divine knowledge and care.

**4. The famous ḥadīth about the Prophet's ﷺ weeping at his mother's grave (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 976).**
Resolved: This ḥadīth is included in the Companion, not the Reader. The Reader narrates the historical event; the Companion draws on this authenticated memory of the Prophet's ﷺ grief and his du'ā' at the grave. The ḥadīth is cited in full in the Companion's Memory Gem.

**5. How to address Āminah's religious status (i.e., whether she accepted Islam)?**
Resolved: This is a matter of scholarly difference and is NOT addressed in this lesson or the Companion. It is a sensitive theological issue that goes beyond the educational scope of this lesson (the life of a six-year-old child). It is noted here as a topic to be treated with extreme care if it ever becomes necessary to address.

## SOURCES ACCEPTED

- Journey to Yathrib and purpose: Ibn Hishām (T2), Ibn Saʿd (T2). Consistent accounts.
- Location of death (al-Abwāʾ): Ibn Hishām (T2). This is the classical position and is not significantly disputed.
- Age at death of Āminah (~6 years): Ibn Hishām (T2). Used with hedging.
- Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 976 (grave visit ḥadīth): Tier 1. Used in Companion, not Reader.

---

## EDITORIAL DECISIONS MADE DURING THIS LESSON

- Virtue: sabr (patience in loss) and tawakkul — established through Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā (93) as the Qur'ānic frame for the orphan experience.
- The lesson does not philosophise about "why" Āminah had to die — it narrates the event and connects it to Qur'ānic acknowledgment. The "why" belongs to the theological discussion (Companion), not the narrative.
- The Companion does not encourage prolonged grief — it balances the Prophet's ﷺ genuine humanity (he wept at his mother's grave as an adult) with the Islamic frame of acceptance and gratitude.
