# SIRAJONE EDUCATIONAL FRAMEWORK
## 05 Source Hierarchy

**Status:** Permanent Reference  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date Established:** June 2026

---

## PURPOSE

This document establishes the authoritative ranking of all sources used in the OUR LEGACY series. Every factual claim in every lesson is assigned a tier based on this hierarchy. The tier determines the confidence level at which the claim may be stated and the type of hedging language required.

---

## TIER 1 — THE INVIOLABLE FOUNDATION

Tier 1 sources are the highest authority. Claims drawn from Tier 1 sources are stated as established fact in the Reader, without hedging.

### T1-A — THE QURʾĀN
- **Status:** The Word of Allah, preserved without alteration.
- **Use:** Direct quotation with Arabic text, translation, and full reference (Surah name, number, verse[s]).
- **Confidence:** Absolute.
- **Restriction:** Verses are always presented in context. A verse is never cited in a way that misrepresents its meaning or occasion of revelation (asbāb al-nuzūl).

### T1-B — ṢAḤĪḤ AL-BUKHĀRĪ
- **Full title:** Al-Jāmiʿ al-Ṣaḥīḥ al-Mukhtaṣar  
- **Author:** Imām Muḥammad ibn Ismāʿīl al-Bukhārī (d. 256 AH / 870 CE)  
- **Arabic title:** الجامع الصحيح المختصر  
- **Status:** The most rigorously authenticated collection of ḥadīth in Islamic scholarship.
- **Use:** Direct quotation or precise paraphrase with ḥadīth number. No hedging required.
- **Note:** Always cite with full ḥadīth number. Do not confuse ḥadīth numbers — e.g., Bukhārī 3 (First Revelation narration by ʿĀʾishah) ≠ Bukhārī 3816 (Prophet's statement about Khadījah after her death).

### T1-C — ṢAḤĪḤ MUSLIM
- **Full title:** Al-Musnad al-Ṣaḥīḥ al-Mukhtaṣar  
- **Author:** Imām Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj al-Nīsābūrī (d. 261 AH / 875 CE)  
- **Arabic title:** المسند الصحيح المختصر  
- **Status:** Second most authoritative ḥadīth collection. Together with Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, these two are referred to as "al-Ṣaḥīḥayn."
- **Use:** Same as Bukhārī. Direct quotation or precise paraphrase with ḥadīth number.

## TIER 2 — CLASSICAL BIOGRAPHICAL AND HISTORICAL SOURCES

Tier 2 sources are the primary records of the Sīrah and early Islamic history. They are widely accepted in classical scholarship but do not carry the same ḥadīth authentication standards as the two Ṣaḥīḥs. Claims from Tier 2 sources require hedging language (see Writing Standards §C2).

### T2-A — SĪRAT IBN HISHĀM
- **Full title:** Al-Sīrah al-Nabawiyyah (abridged from Ibn Isḥāq's original)
- **Author:** Abū Muḥammad ʿAbd al-Mālik ibn Hishām al-Maʿāfirī (d. 218 AH / 833 CE)
- **Arabic title:** السيرة النبوية  
- **Original source:** Sīrat Ibn Isḥāq (Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq, d. 150 AH / 767 CE)
- **Status:** The earliest and most comprehensive biographical account of the Prophet ﷺ. The primary reference for most Sīrah content.
- **Use:** "Ibn Hishām narrates..." / "According to the Sīrat Ibn Hishām..." / "The classical Sīrah records..."
- **Reliability note:** Ibn Isḥāq's chains of narration include some weak narrators. His reliability has been debated but is accepted for general historical narration by the majority of scholars. He is not used for legal rulings.
- **Key details it provides:** Birth narrative, Year of the Elephant, nursing with Ḥalīmah, opening of the chest, death of Āminah, ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib period, Baḥīrā, trade journeys, Khadījah's age and proposal, Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl, and all Meccan period events.

### T2-B — AL-ṬABARĪ — TĀRĪKH AL-RUSUL WAL-MULŪK
- **Full title:** Tārīkh al-Rusul wal-Mulūk (History of the Prophets and Kings)
- **Author:** Imām Abū Jaʿfar Muḥammad ibn Jarīr al-Ṭabarī (d. 310 AH / 923 CE)
- **Arabic title:** تاريخ الرسل والملوك  
- **Status:** The most comprehensive classical history of the Islamic world. Covers prophetic history from Adam ʿalayhi al-salām to the Abbasid period.
- **Use:** "Al-Ṭabarī records..." / "According to al-Ṭabarī..."
- **Reliability note:** Al-Ṭabarī records narrations from multiple sources, including weak and controversial chains, often without judgment. The author notes his own method: he records what has been transmitted and leaves judgment to the reader. Use al-Ṭabarī with awareness that not every narration he records is authenticated.
- **Key use in lessons:** Alternative age traditions (Khadījah at 25 or 28 rather than 40), chronological details, complementary historical context.

### T2-C — IBN SAʿD — AL-ṬABAQĀT AL-KUBRĀ
- **Full title:** Al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrā (The Major Classes)
- **Author:** Abū ʿAbdullah Muḥammad ibn Saʿd al-Baṣrī (d. 230 AH / 845 CE)
- **Arabic title:** الطبقات الكبرى  
- **Status:** A foundational biographical dictionary of the Prophet ﷺ, his Companions, and subsequent generations. Excellent for biographical details of the Ṣaḥābah and Mothers of the Believers.
- **Use:** "Ibn Saʿd records..." / "According to al-Ṭabaqāt..."
- **Key use in lessons:** Profiles of Companions and Mothers of the Believers. Details about Khadījah's lineage, children, and status.

### T2-D — DALĀIL AL-NUBUWWAH (BAYHAQĪ)
- **Full title:** Dalāʾil al-Nubuwwah wa Maʿrifat Aḥwāl Ṣāḥib al-Sharīʿah
- **Author:** Imām Abū Bakr Aḥmad ibn al-Ḥusayn al-Bayhaqī (d. 458 AH / 1066 CE)
- **Arabic title:** دلائل النبوة ومعرفة أحوال صاحب الشريعة  
- **Status:** A major work documenting the signs and proofs of prophethood.
- **Use:** "Al-Bayhaqī narrates..." / "According to the Dalāʾil..."
- **Key use in lessons:** Miraculous signs during the Prophet's childhood and youth (Ḥalīmah's nursing experience, Baḥīrā's recognition, cloud during the trade journey).

## TIER 3 — SCHOLARLY SYNTHESES AND REFERENCE WORKS

Tier 3 sources are scholarly works that synthesise Tier 1 and Tier 2 material. They are not primary sources. They are used for context, guidance on scholarly positions, and as a helpful cross-reference — but a claim is never sourced to Tier 3 alone. Tier 3 references always point back to their primary Tier 1 or 2 sources.

### T3-A — AL-RAḤĪQ AL-MAKHTŪM (THE SEALED NECTAR)
- **Full title:** Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm
- **Author:** Ṣafī al-Raḥmān al-Mubārakpūrī (d. 2006)
- **Arabic title:** الرحيق المختوم  
- **Status:** A modern Arabic Sīrah that won the First Prize in the Muslim World League competition (1979). Widely used in English-speaking Muslim communities.
- **Use:** As a reference to understand the classical position; not as a primary source.
- **Reliability note:** Generally accurate and well-sourced, but popular summaries sometimes omit nuance. Always verify its claims against Tier 1–2.

### T3-B — IBN KATHĪR — AL-BIDĀYAH WAL-NIHĀYAH
- **Full title:** Al-Bidāyah wal-Nihāyah (The Beginning and the End)
- **Author:** Imām Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUmar ibn Kathīr al-Dimashqī (d. 774 AH / 1373 CE)
- **Arabic title:** البداية والنهاية  
- **Status:** A monumental historical work covering from creation to the 14th century CE. Volume 3 covers the Sīrah in detail.
- **Use:** Helpful for cross-referencing Sīrah events and understanding how classical scholars synthesised narrations.
- **Reliability note:** Ibn Kathīr was a skilled ḥadīth scholar who often evaluates the chains of narrations he includes. More reliable than al-Ṭabarī for critical evaluation.

### T3-C — SUNAN ABŪ DĀWŪD / JĀMIʿ AL-TIRMIDHĪ
- **Status:** Tier 3 for general use in this project. The ḥadīth in these collections are of mixed reliability — some ṣaḥīḥ, some ḥasan, some ḍaʿīf. They require verification before use.
- **Use:** Only with explicit identification of the ḥadīth grade and its source narration chain.

## WORKING WITH THE TIER SYSTEM — PRACTICAL RULES

### Rule 1: Assign Tier Before Writing
Before writing any narrative section, identify the source and its tier. Do not write and then look for a source to match.

### Rule 2: Never Upgrade
A narration from Ibn Hishām (Tier 2) cannot be presented with Tier 1 confidence just because it appears to be significant. The tier is determined by the source, not by the importance of the event.

### Rule 3: Conflicting Sources
When Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources conflict, Tier 1 always wins. When two Tier 2 sources conflict, present the dominant/majority position with clear attribution.

When two Tier 2 sources agree, this strengthens the historical credibility of the narration and may be noted.

### Rule 4: Silence in the Sources
When the sources are silent on a detail that a reader might expect — "how old was the Prophet when his father died?" — the Companion's Key Facts section acknowledges the silence: "The classical sources do not specify..."

Silence is acknowledged, not filled with speculation.

### Rule 5: The Ḍaʿīf Narration Rule
A ḍaʿīf (weak) narration is never included as supporting evidence for a historical fact. If it adds useful context, it is clearly identified as "a weak narration, not relied upon for historical fact, but preserved in the tradition."

A mawḍūʿ (fabricated) narration is never referenced in the Reader or Companion under any circumstances.

## CITATION FORMAT

### Qur'ānic Verses
`Surah [Name], [Number]:[Verse(s)]`  
Example: `Surah al-Tawbah, 9:119`

### Ḥadīth (Bukhārī or Muslim)
`Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ḥadīth [Number]`  
`Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Ḥadīth [Number]`  
Example: `Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ḥadīth 3816`

### Classical Sīrah/History Works
`[Author's name/title], [Relevant section or volume]`  
Example: `Sīrat Ibn Hishām, Meccan period`  
Example: `Al-Ṭabarī, Tārīkh, Vol. 2`

### Modern Works
`[Title] by [Author]`  
Example: `Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm by Ṣafī al-Raḥmān al-Mubārakpūrī`
