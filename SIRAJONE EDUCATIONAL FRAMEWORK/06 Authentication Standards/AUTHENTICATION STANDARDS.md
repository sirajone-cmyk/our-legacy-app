# SIRAJONE EDUCATIONAL FRAMEWORK
## 06 Authentication Standards

**Status:** Permanent Reference  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date Established:** June 2026

---

## PURPOSE

Authentication in the OUR LEGACY context does not mean ḥadīth authentication in the technical Islamic sciences sense (that is covered in 05 Source Hierarchy). This document defines the project's working standard for determining whether a claim, narration, or piece of information is reliable enough to use — and at what level of confidence.

These standards exist because the project occupies a specific position: it is not an academic treatise, not a popular summary, and not a formal fatwa. It is educational content for families and students. The standards here reflect that position.

---

## THE THREE-QUESTION TEST

Before any claim is included in the Reader or Companion, it must pass three questions:

### Q1 — What is the source?
Every claim must be traceable to a named source. "It is well-known that..." is not acceptable without identifying who established it as well-known.

### Q2 — What is the tier of that source?
The source is assigned Tier 1, 2, or 3 based on the Source Hierarchy (see 05 Source Hierarchy). The tier determines the language used.

### Q3 — What is the scholarly consensus?
- Is there a dominant position across the classical scholars?
- Are there significant alternative positions?
- Has any position been explicitly rejected by the scholars?

The writing reflects the answers to all three questions.

## AUTHENTICATION IN PRACTICE — WORKED EXAMPLES

### Example 1: The Prophet ﷺ's age at the time of marriage to Khadījah
**Source:** Sīrat Ibn Hishām (Tier 2)  
**Claim:** Twenty-five years old  
**Consensus:** Widely accepted across classical Sīrah scholarship  
**Language in Reader:** "He is twenty-five years old." (stated as historical fact; source noted in Companion Key Facts)  
**Language in Companion:** "Twenty-five years old, according to the classical Sīrah — Sīrat Ibn Hishām drawing on Ibn Isḥāq. This is the widely accepted position across classical scholarship."

### Example 2: Khadījah's age at marriage
**Source:** Sīrat Ibn Hishām (primary, Tier 2) — says 40. Al-Ṭabarī (Tier 2) records alternative of 28 or 25.  
**Claim:** Forty years old (dominant); alternatives exist  
**Consensus:** Majority position is 40; minority positions exist  
**Language in Reader:** "The most widely transmitted classical position is that she is forty years old."  
**Language in Companion:** "The most widely transmitted classical position, found in Ibn Hishām, is that she was forty years old. Al-Ṭabarī and some other narrations suggest a younger age. The classical position of forty is followed by the majority of scholars."

### Example 3: The cloud that shaded the Prophet ﷺ on the trade journey
**Source:** Dalāʾil al-Nubuwwah (Bayhaqī, Tier 2) and Sīrat Ibn Hishām  
**Claim:** A cloud shaded him from the sun  
**Consensus:** Widely transmitted in classical Sīrah tradition; not in the two Ṣaḥīḥs  
**Language in Reader:** "Classical sources report that Maysarah observed something remarkable during the journey: a cloud that followed Muḥammad ﷺ to shade him in the desert heat. This narration is preserved in the classical Sīrah tradition."  
**Language in Companion:** Presented with attribution, not as a verified historical fact at the level of Tier 1.

### Example 4: The exact words of the First Revelation
**Source:** Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ḥadīth 3 (ʿĀʾishah's narration, Tier 1)  
**Claim:** "Iqraʾ bismi rabbika alladhī khalaq..." (exact wording)  
**Consensus:** Authoritative, no dispute on the core text  
**Language in Reader (when this lesson is written):** Quoted directly from Bukhārī 3, presented as authenticated fact.

## AUTHENTICATION OF ḤADĪTH NUMBERS

A recurring risk in this project is citing the wrong ḥadīth number. This has occurred and must be actively guarded against.

### The Bukhārī 3 vs. 3816 Distinction
- **Bukhārī 3** = Narration by ʿĀʾishah raḍiyallāhu ʿanhā about the First Revelation (the Jibrīl visit, the cave, "Iqraʾ").
- **Bukhārī 3816** = Narration about the Prophet ﷺ praising Khadījah after her death: "She believed in me when people disbelieved me..."

These two ḥadīths are in completely different sections of Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and contain entirely different content. They were confused in an early draft of the L10 Companion's Memory Gem, where "3" was typed instead of "3816."

**Standard for prevention:** Before finalising any ḥadīth citation, read back the full ḥadīth text and confirm it matches the cited number. Do not rely on memory of what a ḥadīth number refers to.

## THE "SAID" STANDARD

The word "said" in the Reader text carries specific authentication implications. The following rules govern its use:

### Direct speech — Tier 1 only
"The Prophet ﷺ said: '...'" — direct speech with quotation marks is only used when the quote is from a Tier 1 source (Qur'ān, Bukhārī, Muslim) with the original Arabic available.

### Indirect narration — Tier 1 or strong Tier 2
"The Prophet ﷺ said, in substance, that..." — this construction indicates a paraphrase and may be used for strong Tier 2 narrations with citation.

### "It is narrated" — Tier 2
"It is narrated that he said..." — this construction signals a Tier 2 source.

### "It is reported" — Tier 2 or weaker
"It is reported that..." — used for narrations with more uncertainty about the chain.

### Never fabricated
Under no circumstances is any word attributed to any historical figure that does not appear in an authenticated source.

## THE SCHOLARLY DISAGREEMENT PROTOCOL

When writers encounter scholarly disagreement, the protocol is:

1. **Identify all positions** — do not assume there is one correct answer.
2. **Identify the dominant position** — what do the majority of classical scholars say?
3. **Identify the strength of each position** — is the minority position based on equally strong evidence or on weaker chains?
4. **Present accordingly:**
   - If one position is clearly dominant and the alternatives are minority views: present the dominant position and note the alternatives exist.
   - If two positions are equally supported by classical scholars: present both clearly.
   - If one position has been explicitly rejected by classical scholars: do not include it, or if included, clearly identify it as rejected.
5. **Never force resolution** — if scholars disagree, the text reflects that disagreement honestly.

---

## WHAT AUTHENTICATION DOES NOT MEAN

Authentication in this project does not mean:
- That every lesson must only use Tier 1 sources. That would make the Sīrah impossible to narrate (the two Ṣaḥīḥs contain only fragments of the biographical narrative).
- That Tier 2 sources are unreliable. They are the primary sources for the Sīrah.
- That popular works like Al-Raḥīq al-Makhtūm are forbidden. They are useful synthesis tools but must be verified against Tier 1–2 sources.
- That every claim must have a ḥadīth number. Historical narrations in the Sīrah may not have individual numbers.

Authentication means: every claim has a traceable source, and the language used reflects the reliability of that source.
