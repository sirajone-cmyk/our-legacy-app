## THE MISSION

SirajOne exists to make authentic Islamic education accessible to every Muslim family, classroom, and individual learner. The OUR LEGACY series is its first major expression: a multi-volume educational journey through the lives of the Prophet ﷺ, his Companions, the Mothers of the Believers, and the scholars who preserved and transmitted the Dīn.

The project does not aim to simplify Islam. It aims to make depth accessible — not by removing the complexity of history and scholarship, but by presenting it with clarity, structure, and beauty, in a way that honours both the intelligence of the reader and the sanctity of the subject.

---

## THE NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES

These principles are permanent. They govern every lesson, every Companion, every source note, every editorial decision. They cannot be overridden by convenience, deadline, or user preference.

---

### PRINCIPLE 1 — AUTHENTICITY ABOVE ALL

Every claim in the reader content must be traceable to a specific source of known scholarly standing.

- No content may be included that is not supported by at least one Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 source (see the Source Hierarchy).
- Ḍaʿīf (weak) narrations may only be mentioned if they are explicitly identified as weak and their weakness is explained.
- Mawḍūʿ (fabricated) narrations are never included, even as examples of rejected material.
- Popular stories, folklore, and culturally transmitted narratives that lack scholarly grounding are never presented as historical fact.
- When scholarly disagreement exists, the dominant position is presented clearly, with alternatives acknowledged and attributed.

**The test:** Could this content be defended in front of a qualified Islamic scholar?

---

### PRINCIPLE 2 — INTELLECTUAL HONESTY

The reader must never be misled about the level of certainty of any claim.

- Tier 1 claims (Qur'an, Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim) are stated directly with full confidence.
- Tier 2 claims use hedging language: "classical sources record," "Ibn Hishām narrates," "it is reported that."
- When the date, age, or detail is disputed, the dispute is acknowledged. The dominant scholarly position is followed, and the alternative is noted.
- The word "said" is reserved for direct, authenticated quotation. Paraphrase is always marked as paraphrase.
- Direct quotation from a Companion is never fabricated. If the spirit of what was said is recorded but the exact words are not, this is written as paraphrase with attribution.

**The test:** If a reader asked "how do you know this?" — could the answer be given honestly?

---

### PRINCIPLE 3 — EDUCATIONAL PURPOSE BEFORE AESTHETICS

Every lesson exists to produce understanding, not to produce admiration for the writing.

- Content is never included to make the prose more dramatic if it lacks scholarly support.
- Stylistic choices (sentence rhythm, emotional tone, vivid language) are always subordinate to accuracy.
- The beauty of the writing is in service of the content, not a substitute for it.
- A beautifully written error is more dangerous than an ugly truth.

**The test:** Remove the style. Does the content still stand?

---

### PRINCIPLE 4 — RESPECT THE READER

The OUR LEGACY reader is assumed to be:
- Intelligent.
- Sincere.
- Capable of engaging with complexity.
- Deserving of the full truth, including nuance and scholarly disagreement.

The reader is never talked down to. Complexity is made accessible, not removed. When a historical question is genuinely contested, the reader is told that it is contested — and why.

---

### PRINCIPLE 5 — STRUCTURAL CONTINUITY

Every lesson is part of a larger journey. Each lesson must:

- Connect backward to what the reader already knows.
- Set up what is coming next (the nextLessonPreview).
- Not narrate events that belong to a future lesson.
- Not leave content gaps that the reader will not be able to fill from later lessons.

**The test:** Does this lesson assume knowledge not yet given? Does it narrate something that should be saved for its own lesson?

---

### PRINCIPLE 6 — THE LESSON IS NEVER COMPLETE UNTIL EVERY LAYER IS DONE

A lesson marked "written" is not complete. A lesson is complete only when it has passed every layer of the completion standard (see Checklist 16-A). Reader pages, Companion, Profiles, Maps, References, Discovery links, Authentication check, Deployment, Git, Live Verification, Reader approval — all must pass before the lesson is closed.

---

### PRINCIPLE 7 — THE FIRST REVELATION RULE

The First Revelation (Iqraʾ) is the most significant event in the history of revelation. It must receive its own dedicated lesson. No prior lesson may narrate the First Revelation scene. Lessons that approach the period may anticipate it, but the narration — the cave, the embrace, the words, the trembling, Khadijah's response, Waraqah ibn Nawfal — belongs to and must wait for the dedicated First Revelation lesson.

This principle exists because the weight of that moment requires full attention. Embedding it inside a lesson about something else would diminish it.

---

### PRINCIPLE 8 — THE DEPLOYMENT RULE

The source of truth for content is the OneDrive folder. The source of truth for the live application is C:\Dev\SirajOne\OUR_LEGACY_APP. The deploy script always copies from OneDrive to Dev before building. Deployment scripts always live in C:\Dev\SirajOne\OUR_LEGACY_APP, never in OneDrive. Every deploy is verified live before the lesson is marked complete.

---

### PRINCIPLE 9 — DOCUMENTATION CONTINUITY

Everything that is decided, every standard that is set, every change that is made to an earlier decision — must be documented in this archive before the session ends. If it is only in the chat history, it does not exist.

---

### PRINCIPLE 10 — THE ARCHIVE IS LIVING

This archive is not written once and forgotten. It is updated:
- When a new lesson is completed.
- When a new editorial decision is made.
- When a standard is revised.
- When a deployment workflow changes.
- When a new source is added to the library.

Every significant change to the project is a change to the archive.

---

## THE HIERARCHY OF AUTHORITY

When two principles appear to conflict, this hierarchy resolves it:

1. Authenticity (Principle 1)
2. Intellectual Honesty (Principle 2)
3. Structural Continuity (Principle 5)
4. Educational Purpose (Principle 3)
5. Respect the Reader (Principle 4)
6. Completion Standards (Principle 6)

Style, aesthetics, deadlines, and convenience are always at the bottom of this hierarchy.

---

## WHO THIS FRAMEWORK IS FOR

This framework is written for:

1. **Future contributors** who join the project and need to understand how it works.
2. **The founding author**, who needs a reference when memory fades or chat history is unavailable.
3. **Future educational projects** — Islamic or otherwise — that want to use the same methodology to create a different series.
4. **Quality assurance** — every audit check can be run against this framework.

The framework assumes no prior knowledge of the project. Everything needed to understand the system is inside the archive.

---

## WHAT THIS ARCHIVE DOES NOT DO

- It does not write new lessons.
- It does not improve existing content.
- It does not modify the app.
- It does not serve as a deployment tool.

It is documentation only. Its purpose is preservation, reference, and replication.
